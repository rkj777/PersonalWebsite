About Me

From a young age I have always loved interacting with computer hardware and software, which made the decision to pursue a career in software development an easy one. I have graduated from the University of Alberta with a BSc in Computer Engineering and as I progressed through my studies I began to realize my passion for software development. This caused me to focus on developing my software developer skill set by using nearly all my electives to take interesting and challenging courses that increased my abilities as a developer. On top of this, I have spent numerous hours over the years on personal projects that allowed me to use the skills I have acquired in innovative ways.

The strongest portion of my skill set is definitely my object-oriented design skills, as Java was the first language I worked with extensively and I have spent countless hours researching aspects of OOD such as design patterns. However, the most important trait I possess, in my opinion, is that I love to learn. This trait has caused me to always be fiddling with some new technology that I have picked up. Whether it be solving coding problems with Python, creating Android applications, designing and implementing websites with HTML/CSS, or even just messing around with different JavaScript libraries, I always love to apply the new skills that I learn. Even concepts such as functional programing with Lisp or constraint logic programing with Prolog are tools I love to work with as they allow me to think in different ways. In summary, learning and evolving as a software developer is an important part of my life and as I go forward in my career I know my thirst for knowledge will never wane because developing software is just too much fun.

Gesture Control Interface Glove

For the final capstone project of my Computer Engineering degree, two colleagues and I created a gesture control interface glove. This glove allowed a user to execute keyboard shortcuts on a windows computer with hand gestures. Specifically, alt-tab/alt-shift-tab were implemented to switch tabs and page up and page down were implemented to scroll through documents. This project involved many aspects of Computer Engineering and the portions of the project I developed included:
  • Circuit design and implementation
  • Microcontroller and embedded systems programming, specifically programing the ATmega328p located on the glove
  • FPGA programming, specifically programming the Altera DE2
  • Programming the gesture recognition algorithm used the to determine the gesture the user invoked

Shiny Expense Tracker

Shiny Expense Tracker was an Android application I developed as part of a team of six people. The application was aimed at companies as it allowed employees to keep track of expenses that would be covered by the company. To do this managers and employees would create accounts and employees would send their manager various expenses to approve. These expenses could have additional data attached such as location information and receipt images.

This project allowed me to gain invaluable experience with many aspects of software engineering. Specifically working on such a large project with a team of five others allowed me to exponentially increase my abilities to work effectively as part of a team. The more technical skills that I had to use to develop this project included:
  • Android app development with Java
  • Agile software development
  • Effective use of object-oriented design and object-oriented design patterns
  • Version control with Git and GitHub
  • Unit testing
  • Test-driven development
  • Working with APIs
  • Elasticsearch
